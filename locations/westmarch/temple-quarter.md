# Temple Quarter

The Temple Quarter is the spiritual and scholarly heart of [Westmarch](/locations/westmarch/overview), located northeast of [Oldtown](/locations/westmarch/oldtown). All five major temples are here, along with the city's Archive. The streets are clean, well-lit, and peaceful. This is where citizens come seeking healing, justice, knowledge, or divine guidance.

**Population:** ~2,500

## Overview

The Temple Quarter was established in the decades following the Green War, when the city's reconstruction included dedicating an entire district to religious institutions. The temples here represent the five deities most important to Westmarch's culture and survival: Bahamut (protection and justice), Silvanus (nature and balance), Erathis (civilization and law), Avandra (travel and luck), and Ioun (knowledge and study).

The district maintains a peaceful, contemplative atmosphere. Street lamps are kept burning through the night, and the Watch maintains a strong presence to ensure safety. Pilgrims and scholars from across the kingdom visit the temples and Archive, making this one of the more cosmopolitan parts of the city.

## Key Locations

### The Platinum Basilica

The grand temple of Bahamut, god of justice, protection, and honor.

**Architecture:**
- Impressive domed structure with platinum-leafed spires
- Stained glass windows depicting heroic legends
- Main sanctuary can hold hundreds of worshippers
- Multiple chapels for private prayer

**Functions:**
- Daily religious services and ceremonies
- Hospital wing providing healing to all who need it
- Orphanage caring for children who've lost families
- Training yards where paladins and clerics practice
- Administrative offices for temple operations

**Leadership:** The Basilica is led by high-ranking clergy devoted to Bahamut's ideals of justice and protection.

### The Oak Father's Grove

The temple of Silvanus, god of nature, wilderness, and the cycle of seasons.

**Architecture:**
- Incorporates living trees into its structure
- Open-air sections with gardens and natural pools
- Built around ancient oaks that predate the temple
- Stone and wood construction blending with nature

**Functions:**
- Seasonal rituals honoring natural cycles
- Gardens cultivating medicinal and ritual herbs
- Herbalist workshops producing remedies and potions
- Counseling for those seeking balance with nature
- Meditation spaces among the trees

**Significance:** The temple maintains a delicate position regarding the Thornwood and the Greenwood Pact. Its clergy try to honor nature while supporting the city's safety.

### The Hall of Law

The temple of Erathis, goddess of civilization, law, and community.

**Architecture:**
- Formal stone building with classical columns
- Clean lines and orderly design
- Hall of records with carefully maintained documents
- Courtrooms and arbitration chambers

**Functions:**
- Courthouse for civil disputes
- Arbitration center for merchant conflicts
- Legal counseling and advocacy
- Witness to official contracts and agreements
- Repository of legal precedents

**Leadership:** Presided over by judges and clerics who serve as both religious leaders and legal authorities.

### The Wayfarer's Chapel

The welcoming temple of Avandra, goddess of travel, trade, luck, and change.

**Architecture:**
- Open, airy design with large doors
- Decorated with symbols from many lands
- Space for travelers to rest and pray
- Notice board for travelers and merchants

**Functions:**
- Blessings for travelers beginning journeys
- Sanctuary for those far from home
- Information hub for trade routes and road conditions
- Small shrine to leave offerings for safe passage
- Counseling for those facing uncertain choices

**Character:** The most welcoming and accessible temple, often visited by merchants, adventurers, and refugees seeking hope.

### The Archive of Ioun

The three-story library and temple complex dedicated to Ioun, goddess of knowledge, prophecy, and study.

**Architecture:**
- Sturdy stone construction with extensive shelving
- Reading rooms with good lighting
- Scriptorium where scribes copy texts
- Private study chambers for scholars
- Secure basement vault

**Leadership:** High Archivist Elara oversees the Archive and its collections.

#### The Archive's Three Levels

**Ground Floor: The Public Library**
- Open to all citizens
- Standard books, histories, and references
- Common knowledge and educational texts
- Helpful but not dangerous information
- Staffed by assistant librarians

**Second Floor: The Restricted Stacks**
- Requires permission from Elara or senior clergy
- More specialized and scholarly texts
- Some magical books requiring careful handling
- Historical records from the Green War
- Access granted to serious scholars and researchers

**Basement: The Vault**
- Very restricted (Elara personally decides who enters)
- Oldest and most dangerous texts
- Some written in languages few can read
- Contains fragmentary references to [the Binding](/lore/the-binding) and the Greenheart
- Original documents from the ritual participants
- Not complete information, but enough to piece together the truth
- Houses the [Archive Stone](/lore/anchor-stones/archive-stone), one of the seven Anchor Stones

### Temple Square

A large open plaza at the center of the district where all five temple paths converge.

**Functions:**
- Religious festivals and public ceremonies
- Seasonal celebrations
- Gathering space for the community
- Market during festival days
- Public speeches and announcements

**Atmosphere:** A place where the divine presence feels close, where citizens of different faiths mingle peacefully, and where the city's spiritual life is most visible.

## Current Tensions

The Temple Quarter has not escaped the current crisis. The clergy of Silvanus wrestle with questions about the Greenheart and humanity's relationship with the forest. The Archive's vault contains secrets that some believe should be revealed, while others insist must remain hidden. And all five temples are seeing increased attendance as fearful citizens seek divine protection from the growing darkness.
