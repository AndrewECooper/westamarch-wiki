# Session 4: Questions and Alliances

**Date:** Greenleaf 25th-28th, 1247

## Quill's Research

Back in [Westmarch](/locations/westmarch), [Quill](/characters/player-characters/quill) threw himself into research at [the Archive](/locations/westmarch/the-archive), desperate to find an alternative to venturing into [Harrow Keep](/locations/thornwood/harrow-keep) and its potential dragon.

His digging paid off. He discovered that the Tess-Kydra was originally an artifact from **Celindara**, an ancient elven city swallowed by [the Thornwood](/locations/thornwood) during [the Green War](/history/the-green-war). Old maps in the Archive still noted its location—deep in the forest, a week or two's journey from the city.

[Quill](/characters/player-characters/quill) wondered whether Celindara itself might hold answers, perhaps another way to recharge [the Warren Stone](/lore/anchor-stones/warren-stone) without needing the Tess-Kydra at all.

## Lyrique's Doubts

[Lyrique](/characters/player-characters/lyrique) sought out [Lord Brennan](/characters/npcs/lord-brennan), her patron, but for the first time came with questions rather than just reports. She told him about the encounter with [Lady Oona](/characters/npcs/lady-oona) at [the Singing Pools](/locations/thornwood/the-singing-pools) and about [Kaelen's](/characters/player-characters/kaelen-vale) vision of the Duke.

[Brennan](/characters/npcs/lord-brennan) claimed not to recognize the wardstone from the vision. He cautioned [Lyrique](/characters/player-characters/lyrique) that visions and words from the Fey were not to be trusted—that the Fey didn't think like humans and didn't share the same moral framework.

Small doubts were beginning to form, though [Lyrique](/characters/player-characters/lyrique) kept them mostly to herself.

## Kaelen and the Archdruid

[Kaelen](/characters/player-characters/kaelen-vale) visited [Archdruid Theron](/characters/npcs/theron-mossbark) and learned the name of the fey woman from the Pools: [**Lady Oona**](/characters/npcs/lady-oona). [Theron](/characters/npcs/theron-mossbark) warned that Oona was quite dangerous.

The Archdruid also explained that the Fey Folk held a very different view of [the Greenwood Pact](/lore/the-greenwood-pact) than the humans did—a tension that ran deeper than the party had understood.

When [Kaelen](/characters/player-characters/kaelen-vale) pressed about the wardstones, [Theron](/characters/npcs/theron-mossbark) admitted he had knowledge of them but wasn't comfortable sharing it yet. He didn't trust all of [Kaelen's](/characters/player-characters/kaelen-vale) companions. The implication was clear: he didn't trust [Lyrique](/characters/player-characters/lyrique), because of her patron.

## The Meeting at the Archive

The entire party convened at [the Archive](/locations/westmarch/the-archive) to discuss their findings and make a plan. During the meeting, [Lady Sera](/characters/npcs/lady-sera)—[Quill's](/characters/player-characters/quill) friend and daughter of the Duke—arrived and joined the conversation.

The party tried to gauge obliquely whether [Duke Aldric](/characters/npcs/duke-aldric) knew what was happening with [the stones](/lore/anchor-stones/overview). [Sera](/characters/npcs/lady-sera) proved sharper than they expected and saw through their questioning immediately.

She didn't give them what they wanted, but she offered a deal: she would arrange an audience with [the Duke](/characters/npcs/duke-aldric), but only if they agreed to take her with them to [Harrow Keep](/locations/thornwood/harrow-keep).

They agreed.
