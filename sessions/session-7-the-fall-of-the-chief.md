# Session 7: The Fall of the Chief

**Date:** Greenleaf 30th–31st, 1247

## Interrogating the Prisoners

In the aftermath of the kobold ambush, the party turned to the four prisoners they had taken. They isolated the largest, a kobold named **Torque**, and began questioning him about [Harrow Keep](/locations/thornwood/harrow-keep) and the dragon they served.

Torque confirmed what [Elara Moonwhisper](/characters/npcs/elara-moonwhisper) had warned them of: the kobolds of the Poison Scale worship [Verdanthis](/characters/npcs/verdanthis) as a god, and she lairs at their castle—Harrow Keep itself. He boasted of "many" kobolds and a large nursery of eggs guarded within. There were no secret entrances, he said; the only way in was past their leader, **Chief Scris "The Claw."**

[Quill](/characters/player-characters/quill) raised a magical *Haven* for the long rest, and during the watches [Lyrique](/characters/player-characters/lyrique) sat with Torque and played a cup-and-ball guessing game, deliberately letting him win a few coppers to build rapport. Loosened by the small kindness, Torque revealed that the Poison Scale frequently clashed with **"dog men"**—gnolls—who threatened their hunting grounds.

A plan formed: the party would offer Chief Scris an exchange. The Poison Scale's gnoll problem, in return for the Tess-Kydra.

## Harrow Keep

Following their rest, Torque led the party along a hidden trail to [Harrow Keep](/locations/thornwood/harrow-keep). The fortress was as ruined as the histories described—walls draped in ivy and split by roots, a rusted portcullis hanging askew, an old well, and a great tree growing up through the central courtyard.

Chief Scris "The Claw" met them in the courtyard, surrounded by his warriors and advisors. [Sera](/characters/npcs/lady-sera) and [Lyrique](/characters/player-characters/lyrique) handled the negotiations, offering to deal with the gnolls in exchange for the crucible. Scris consulted quietly with his lieutenants, who eyed the heavily-armed strangers with suspicion.

In the back-and-forth, the chief let something slip: *"the great one left the bowl in our charge."* The party caught it immediately. Verdanthis was not at Harrow Keep. She had left the Tess-Kydra here for the Poison Scale to guard—but the dragon herself was elsewhere.

It didn't save the negotiation. Unwilling to surrender the relic he had been entrusted with, and likely fearing that letting one band of adventurers walk away with it would only invite more, Scris cut the talks short and ordered his warriors to attack.

## The Battle in the Courtyard

[Quill](/characters/player-characters/quill) acted first, hurling a second-level *Freeze* across the courtyard. A swath of ground iced over into difficult terrain and several kobolds were caught and held in the freezing surge.

The kobolds fought back viciously. Several of them unleashed a poison breath weapon—gouts of green ichor that scoured [Wynn](/characters/npcs/wynn-copper) and [Kaelen](/characters/player-characters/kaelen-vale). One kobold warrior closed on [Sera](/characters/npcs/lady-sera) with a longsword and dropped her unconscious before the party could intervene. [Drakov](/characters/player-characters/drakov) called on Bahamut and brought her back with *Healing Word*.

[Quill](/characters/player-characters/quill) followed with a *Sleep* spell, dropping three more kobolds into the dirt. From the cover of the courtyard's overgrown shrubs and the great central tree, [Thalia](/characters/player-characters/thalia) picked off enemies one by one with deadly sneak attacks from her shortbow.

A kobold spellcaster turned on [Quill](/characters/player-characters/quill) and struck him with *Magic Missile*. The fairy was knocked unconscious and tumbled out of the air, failing a death save in the fall. [Drakov](/characters/player-characters/drakov) called *Healing Word* a second time and saved him.

The turning point came when [Drakov](/characters/player-characters/drakov) waded toward Chief Scris and brought his mace down in a devastating critical hit—crushing the chief outright.

## Aftermath

Their leader dead, the surviving kobolds broke and tried to flee. [Lyrique](/characters/player-characters/lyrique) caught the dangerous spellcaster mid-flight with *Hold Person*. [Wynn](/characters/npcs/wynn-copper) rushed across the courtyard and hogtied the paralyzed sorcerer where he stood.

With the chief dead, the sorcerer bound, and the rest of the kobolds either frozen, sleeping, or fled, the party stood victorious in the courtyard of [Harrow Keep](/locations/thornwood/harrow-keep). The Tess-Kydra was here, somewhere in the keep—and the dragon, by the chief's own words, was not.

What to do with the prisoners, and how to find the relic in the ruins, would have to wait for next session.
