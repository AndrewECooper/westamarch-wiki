# Lord Brennan Westmarch

## Role
Duke's younger brother, political figure

## Race and Age
Human, 49 years old

## Location
Estate in Northbank, frequently at court

## Description
Charismatic and popular—especially with common folk, merchants, and younger nobles—Brennan speaks passionately about "Westmarch's future" and "opportunities for all." He argues the city must be allowed to grow and that [the Pact](/lore/the-greenwood-pact), while important, should evolve with the times. Many see him as a voice of progress and compassion, particularly his support for refugees and the poor.

## What He Knows
**[Tier 4+ knowledge](/lore/knowledge-tiers)**: Brennan knows the truth about [the Binding](/lore/the-binding) and the [Anchor Stones](/lore/anchor-stones/overview). His knowledge extends beyond what most in House Westmarch understand.

## Public Persona
Lord Brennan presents himself as a progressive reformer who cares deeply about Westmarch's future. He is known for:
- Passionate advocacy for the common people
- Support for refugees and the poor
- Arguing that the Greenwood Pact should evolve with modern times
- Speaking about "opportunities for all" and the city's potential for growth
- Popularity with merchants, younger nobles, and the working class

## Current State
Actively engaged in political life, maintaining his estate in Northbank and making frequent appearances at court. He maintains a public image of compassionate progressivism while privately working toward his own agenda.

## Motivation
Complex and not fully revealed. His public statements emphasize growth, opportunity, and modernization of Westmarch's relationship with the Thornwood.

## Connections and Relationships
- **Brother**: [Duke Aldric Westmarch](/characters/npcs/duke-aldric)
- **Sister-in-law**: [Duchess Elara Westmarch](/characters/npcs/duchess-elara)
- **Niece**: [Lady Sera Westmarch](/characters/npcs/lady-sera)
- **Faction**: [House Westmarch](/factions/house-westmarch)
- **Known Associates**: Has connections throughout Westmarch's political and merchant classes

## Estate
Lord Brennan maintains an estate in Northbank, which serves as his primary residence and a gathering place for his political supporters.

## Suspicions and Tensions
There are hints of tension within House Westmarch, though the exact nature is not public knowledge. Some traditionalists at court are wary of Brennan's calls for change, while others see him as the future of the city's leadership.
